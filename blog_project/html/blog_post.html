A new beginning

In the summer and the autumn of 1185 once again the Balkans have become a stage for the clash of world powers. The Sicilian Normans invaded in July and sacked Thessaloica a month later. This to led to a chaos in the Byzantine empire resulting in a revolt during which the throne was overtaken by Isaac Angelos II. Could one imagine a better times for a nation endured the tests of history to rise again for a new start.

October, 1185, Cypsela

The art of becoming a doer but not just a mere actor is hard to master. It takes more than pure courage and the goodwill of fate to consciously feel that in this very moment you are one with the universe and do what you have to. Two brothers Asen and Peter, descendants of the once-great Bulgarian empire tzars, are just about to enter the room for an audience with Isaac II, the Byzantium emperor himself. Some people say that when a great thing is about to happen a prepared person with his senses well-tuned and trained can feel it. Inside the room, there is the emperor, his uncle John Doukas (who is also now his adviser), a translator and funny looking chronicler wearing shabby clothes, and several bored and emotionless soldiers. The brothers took a deep breath and entered a spacious room. Last time when they've been honoured with the chance for an audience with the emperor the crown, with its magically gleaming sapphires and diamonds, was worn by Andronikos I Komnenos. Isaac II overthrew him just months ago. Now the two brothers looked and noded at each other, taking a deep breath approached the new emperor, bowed a bit and waited for permission to speak.
